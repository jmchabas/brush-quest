# B2B Opportunities — Parked for Phase 3+
**Last updated**: 2026-03-14
**Status**: Research complete. Execution parked until >$100K ARR.

## Why B2B Matters

B2B2C (through insurers, dentists, schools) is the path from $1M to $10M+ ARR. But it requires sales infrastructure, a separate product layer, and a proven consumer base. Pursue after consumer model is validated.

## Channel 1: Dental Insurance Companies

### The Value Proposition
Insurers save $88-$156/child/year when preventive care avoids caries (6-state Medicaid study, 930K children tracked over 6 years). Every $1 on prevention saves $8-$50 in restorative treatment.

### Model: Beam Dental (Proof of Concept)
Beam Dental ships connected toothbrushes to members, tracks brushing via Bluetooth, adjusts premiums based on a "wellness score." Members earn points for brushing. Raised $170M+ in funding.

Brush Quest could be the SOFTWARE-ONLY version of Beam's model — no hardware needed.

### Revenue Model
- Per-member-per-month (PMPM) fee: $1-$3 for families with children
- One insurer with 100K child members at $1 PMPM = $1.2M/year ARR
- Target insurers: Delta Dental, DentaQuest (2M Texas Medicaid members), Cigna, MetLife

### What's Needed
- Enterprise sales capability (or a partnership)
- Outcomes dashboard for insurers (aggregate brushing compliance data)
- White-label or co-branded version
- Clinical study or pilot data showing improved brushing compliance
- HIPAA considerations (if handling insurer member data)

### Effort: HIGH. Timeline: 6-12 months from first conversation to revenue.

## Channel 2: Pediatric Dentist Offices

### The Value Proposition
- 8,000+ pediatric dentists in the US
- Each sees hundreds of families per year
- A patient who brushes = fewer emergency visits, better outcomes, happier parents = referrals
- Dentists already recommend brushing apps (Brush DJ, Chomper Chums) for free

### Revenue Model
"Dentist Partner Plan" at $49-$99/month:
- 50-100 patient referral codes per month (each gives family free premium trial)
- Practice dashboard showing patient engagement
- Co-branded recommendation cards for waiting room
- Dentist gets "recommended by Dr. Smith" branding in the app

### TAM
8,000 pediatric dentists x $79/month average = $7.6M/year at full penetration.
Realistic near-term: 100 practices at $79/month = $95K ARR.

### What's Needed
- Separate "Dentist Portal" (could be a simple web dashboard)
- Patient referral code system
- Sales outreach (email campaigns, dental conference attendance)
- Pilot with 5-10 local dentists to prove the model

### Effort: MEDIUM. Timeline: 3-6 months.

## Channel 3: Schools & Head Start Programs

### The Value Proposition
- Head Start programs (1,600+ sites, ~1M children) are **federally mandated** to ensure children brush after meals
- CDC funds 15 states at ~$380K/year each for oral health promotion
- School-based dental programs operate on ~$500K/year budgets
- Existing curricula (Colgate Bright Smiles, Cavity Free Kids) are print-based, not interactive

### Revenue Model
Site license at $500-$2,000/school/year (less than $5/student):
- Classroom challenge features (teacher dashboard, class leaderboard)
- Take-home app access for families
- Brushing compliance reporting for grant requirements

### TAM
Head Start: 1,600 programs x $1,000/year average = $1.6M/year
Plus school districts: potentially $5M+ TAM

### What's Needed
- "Classroom" version with teacher dashboard
- Grant-friendly pricing documentation
- Outreach to Head Start directors, school nurses
- Long sales cycles (6-12 months for institutional buyers)

### Effort: HIGH. Timeline: 12+ months.

## Channel 4: Toothbrush/Toothpaste Brands

### The Landscape
| Brand | Kids Product | App Strategy |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Colgate | hum Kids ($15-20) | Own mediocre AR app |
| Oral-B | Kids battery brush ($8-15) | Disney Magic Timer (licensed) |
| Philips | Sonicare for Kids ($30-50) | Own companion app |
| Hello Products | Kids toothpaste | No app |
| Tom's of Maine | Kids toothpaste | No app |

### Opportunity
- Colgate and Oral-B have apps but they're mediocre compared to Brush Quest
- Hello Products and Tom's of Maine have no digital strategy
- A co-branded "Brush Quest x [Brand]" toothbrush ($15-20) with premium app access code could work

### Revenue Model
- Licensing deal: $100K-$500K upfront + royalties per unit
- Or: co-development partnership with revenue share
- Or: bundle code insertion (brand buys premium codes at $2-3 each, includes in product packaging)

### What's Needed
- Business development relationship with brand marketing teams
- Proven consumer traction (downloads, ratings, retention data)
- Willingness to co-brand or white-label

### Effort: HIGH. Timeline: 6-18 months.

## Channel 5: White-Label Licensing

### Typical Pricing
- Setup fee: $10K-$30K
- Monthly SaaS: $500-$5K/month
- Deep customization: $20K-$100K+

### Potential Buyers
| Buyer | Use Case | Revenue |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Dental DSOs (large chains) | Patient engagement tool branded to practice | $500-$2K/location/month |
| Children's hospitals | Post-visit oral health engagement | One-time license + maintenance |
| Government health departments | Medicaid member engagement | Grant-funded per-child licensing |

### Effort: VERY HIGH. Not viable as a solo developer.

## Recommended Sequencing

1. **Now → Phase 2**: Consumer model only. Prove retention and conversion.
2. **At $100K ARR**: Pilot with 5 local pediatric dentists. Low cost, high signal.
3. **At $250K ARR**: Approach one dental insurer for a pilot (DentaQuest or a regional plan).
4. **At $500K ARR**: Hire Head of Partnerships. Pursue brand deals and school programs.
5. **At $1M ARR**: Full B2B2C operation with enterprise sales.
