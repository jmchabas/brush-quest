# Pricing Research — Value-Based Analysis
**Last updated**: 2026-03-14

## The Core Insight

Brush Quest was initially priced as a GAME competing with other games ($0-$3.99). It should be priced as a PARENTING OUTCOME TOOL competing with tools that solve daily battles ($5-$15/month). However, the parent-feature evaluation (see `parent-features-evaluation.md`) revealed that the proposed parent features don't deliver enough value to justify outcome-tool pricing alone. The recommended hybrid is content-led at game-adjacent pricing ($2.99/mo).

## Dental Cost Economics

### The "Costs Less Than One Filling" Value Prop

| Dental Procedure | Cost (without insurance) | Cost (with insurance) |
|-----------------|--------------------------|----------------------|
| Silver filling | $50-$150/tooth | $20-$60/tooth |
| Composite filling | $90-$300/tooth | $35-$120/tooth |
| Pediatric crown | $300-$600/tooth | $100-$250/tooth |
| Dental exam + cleaning | $75-$200/visit | $0-$50/visit |
| Emergency visit | $200-$500+ | $100-$250 |
| Dental sedation (young kids) | $150-$500/session | $75-$250/session |

### Cavity Prevalence in Children
- 23% of children ages 2-5 have had dental caries
- **52% of children ages 6-8** have had decay in primary teeth
- 17% of children ages 6-11 have caries in permanent teeth
- Low-income children: 2x the cavity rate
- Source: NIDCR/NIH

### Annual Dental Costs Per Child
- Preventive only (2 visits): $150-$400/year
- One cavity: add $100-$300
- Multiple cavities/crowns: $500-$2,000+/year
- Family dental insurance: $30-$60/month ($360-$720/year)
- UK lifetime per-person dental caries cost: ~$22,910

### The Math
$24.99/year for Brush Quest vs. one filling at $100-$300.
Even if the app prevents one cavity per year, it delivers 4-12x ROI.

## Willingness-to-Pay Spectrum

Based on cross-category research:

| Category | WTP Range | What parents are buying |
|----------|-----------|------------------------|
| Entertainment apps | $0-$5/month | Fun. Easily replaced. |
| Educational apps | $5-$10/month | Learning outcomes. |
| Behavior/habit tools | $5-$15/month | Getting kids to do things. |
| Health outcome tools | $10-$25/month | Concrete health results. |
| Acute pain-point (sleep) | $10-$15/mo or $80-$180 one-time | Solving crisis-level daily pain. |

Key finding: **Outcome positioning > feature positioning.** Sleep apps charge 2-3x educational apps because they solve an OUTCOME, not provide a feature.

| Tier | Monthly | Annual | Effective Monthly |

| Free | $0 | $0 | $0 |
| Space Ranger Pass | $2.99 | $24.99 | $2.08 |

### Why $2.99/mo, Not Higher

1. **Market context**: Every competitor is free. $2.99 is already the most expensive toothbrushing app.
2. **Parent features don't justify $5.99**: The evaluation showed most proposed parent features are weak (see `parent-features-evaluation.md`).
3. **Content-led, not outcome-led**: Until brushing verification is real (requires hardware partner), the value is "more game content" not "proof your kid brushed."
4. **Impulse buy territory**: Parents seeing genuine kid engagement will pay $2.99 without deliberation.
5. **Annual incentive strong**: $24.99/year = 30% discount vs monthly. Annual plans retain 50-60% vs ~25% monthly.

### Future Price Increases
Once proven with data:
- Add genuine parent features (real verification via smart toothbrush partner) → justify $5.99-$7.99/mo
- Family plan ($4.99/mo for up to 5 kids) → higher ARPU from multi-child families
- B2B pricing (dental insurer per-member) → $1-3 PMPM, different channel entirely
