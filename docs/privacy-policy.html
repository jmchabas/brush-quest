Brush Quest is a toothbrushing game for kids aged 5 and up, built by a dad for his own kids and shared with other families. We designed it to be the kind of app we'd want on our own children's devices. This policy explains what data we handle, how we protect it, and your rights as a parent.

This policy complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), including the 2025 amendments, and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA).

1. What We Collect

Game Progress (stored on your device)

The app stores gameplay data locally to track your child's brushing habits and in-game progress:

  • Brush count, brush history (dates only), and streaks
  • Stars earned, unlocked heroes and weapons
  • Settings preferences (timer duration, sound, camera toggle)

This data stays on the device unless a parent opts in to cloud saving.

Cloud Save (optional, parent-controlled)

A parent may sign in with Google to back up game progress. This feature is behind a parental gate (math verification) and requires you to tap "I Consent" before any data is sent. When signed in, we access:

  • Your email address — to identify your cloud save
  • Your display name — shown in Settings so you know which account is connected

No profile photo, contacts, or other Google data is accessed. Google Sign-In is for parents only — children cannot sign in without passing the parental gate.

Camera (optional, off by default)

Brush Quest can use the front camera to detect brushing motion during gameplay. This feature is disabled by default and requires a parent to enable it in Settings.

  • Camera frames are processed entirely on the device in real time
  • Each frame is reduced to a tiny grid and produces a single motion score — no facial recognition, no biometrics, no identification
  • No images or video are ever stored, recorded, or sent anywhere

Crash Reports (automatic, anonymous)

We collect anonymous crash reports via Firebase Crashlytics to fix bugs. These include device model, OS version, and technical error details. No personal information is included.

Usage Analytics (anonymous)

We collect anonymous usage data via Firebase Analytics, configured for child-directed treatment under COPPA. This data is sent to Google's servers as a service provider acting on our behalf — not as an independent third party. Google is contractually prohibited from using this data for its own purposes.

  • Events like brush completion, onboarding progress, and shop visits
  • No advertising IDs are collected (explicitly disabled)
  • No ad personalization, remarketing, or profiling
  • Data is anonymous and aggregated — it cannot identify any individual child
  • Firebase uses installation IDs solely for internal operations as permitted under COPPA (16 CFR 312.5(c)(7)) — not for contacting individuals or building profiles
  • We do not share children's data with third parties for commercial, advertising, or AI training purposes

Website Email List (optional)

On our website (brushquest.app), visitors may provide their email to receive launch notifications and occasional updates. This is voluntary, not part of the app, and managed by Buttondown (a privacy-focused email service with no tracking pixels or cookies). Every email includes an unsubscribe link.

2. What We Don't Collect

Brush Quest does not collect:

  • Names or ages of children
  • Location data (GPS, IP-based, or otherwise)
  • Contacts, phone numbers, or messages
  • Photos, videos, or audio recordings (camera motion detection produces a number, not an image)
  • Advertising IDs or any identifiers used for ad targeting
  • Browsing history or activity outside the app

We do not use any advertising SDKs, social media integrations, or third-party tracking services.

5. Children's Privacy (COPPA)

Brush Quest is designed for children and complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), including the 2025 amendments.

How we protect children

  • We do not collect personal information from children
  • All data collection features (cloud save, camera) require explicit parental consent
  • The camera is disabled by default and produces only a motion score — not biometric data
  • Children cannot make their information publicly available
  • We never condition a child's participation on disclosing more data than necessary
  • There are no social features, no chat, no links to external sites within the app

How we get parental consent

  • Parental gate: Settings (including cloud save and camera) are behind a math verification problem that deters unsupervised access by children
  • Consent dialog: Before enabling cloud save, a dialog explains what data will be stored and links to this privacy policy. You must tap "I Consent" to proceed
  • Camera notice: Before enabling the motion camera, a notice explains its function and confirms no images are stored
  • Separate consent for third-party sharing: Under COPPA (16 CFR 312.5(a)(2)), you may consent to our collection and use of data without consenting to disclosure to third parties. Since we only share data with service providers acting on our behalf (not independent third parties), this consent is bundled with the cloud save consent. If you do not enable cloud save, no data leaves the device (except anonymous crash reports and analytics, which qualify as internal operations under 16 CFR 312.5(c)(7))

Your rights as a parent

Under COPPA, you have the right to:

  • Review your child's data — Game progress is visible on the home screen and in Settings. Cloud data is accessible by signing in. You can also email us for a full summary
  • Delete all data — Use "Delete child's data" in the app's Settings. This erases all local data and all cloud data. You can also request deletion by email
  • Refuse further collection — Sign out of Google to stop cloud syncing. Disable the camera in Settings. The app works fully offline
  • Opt out of analytics — Email us at privacy@brushquest.app to request opt-out

We respond to all parental requests within 30 days.

6. California Privacy Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

AnemosGP LLC is a California company. While we may not currently meet the CCPA revenue thresholds, we provide these disclosures in the spirit of transparency.

Categories of personal information collected in the past 12 months

  • Identifiers: Parent email address and display name (cloud save only, with consent)
  • Internet/electronic activity: Anonymous app usage events, crash reports

We do not collect: real names or ages of children, geolocation, financial information, biometric data, browsing history outside the app, or protected classifications.

Your California rights

  • Right to know — You can request what personal information we've collected about you
  • Right to delete — You can request deletion of your personal information (in-app via Settings, or by email)
  • Right to opt-out of sale/sharing — We do not sell or share personal information as defined by the CCPA. There is nothing to opt out of
  • Right to non-discrimination — We will not treat you differently for exercising your privacy rights

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@brushquest.app. We will verify your identity and respond within 45 days.
