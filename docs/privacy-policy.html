Brush Quest is a children's toothbrushing game designed for kids aged 5 and older. We take your child's privacy seriously. This policy explains what information the app uses, how it is handled, and your rights as a parent or guardian. This policy complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), as amended in 2025.

The short version: Brush Quest has no ads, no location tracking, and no behavioral profiling. The camera is disabled by default and processes motion locally only. We collect anonymous usage analytics (e.g. brush completion rates) to improve the app, with no advertising IDs or personal data. We do not sell or share personal data with anyone.

1. What Information We Collect and Why

Camera (Motion Detection)

Brush Quest can use the device's front camera to detect brushing motion during gameplay. This feature is disabled by default and must be explicitly enabled by a parent or guardian in Settings.

  • Camera frames are processed entirely on the device in real time.
  • The processing reduces each frame to a 32×32 pixel grid and computes aggregate motion intensity (a single number). No facial recognition, facial template extraction, or biometric identification is performed.
  • No images or video are ever stored, recorded, or transmitted.
  • The motion detection output cannot be used to identify any individual.
  • A consent notice is shown before the camera can be enabled.

Google Sign-In (Optional, Parent-Operated)

A parent or guardian may choose to sign in with a Google account to enable cloud saving. This feature is behind a parental gate (math verification) and requires explicit data consent before sign-in. When signed in, we access:

  • Email address — used solely to identify the user's cloud save data.
  • Display name — shown on the settings screen so the parent can confirm which account is connected.

No profile photo, contacts, or other Google account data is accessed. Google Sign-In is intended for parents or guardians only, not for children. The parental verification gate and explicit consent dialog serve as verifiable parental consent under COPPA.

Game Progress Data

The app stores gameplay information to track your child's brushing habits and in-game rewards:

  • Total brush count and brush history (dates only)
  • Current streak and best streak
  • Stars earned
  • Unlocked heroes and weapons
  • Selected hero and weapon
  • Settings preferences (timer duration, sound, camera toggle)

Crash Reports (Firebase Crashlytics)

The app collects anonymous crash reports to help us fix bugs and improve stability. These reports include:

  • Device model and operating system version
  • Stack traces (technical error details)
  • App version

Crash reports contain no personal information such as names, emails, or gameplay data.

Usage Analytics (Firebase Analytics)

The app collects anonymous usage data to help us understand how children use the app and improve the experience. This is configured for child-directed treatment under COPPA:

  • Events tracked: Brush session start/completion/abandonment, onboarding completion, daily logins, shop visits, hero and weapon unlocks.
  • Aggregate properties: Lifetime brush count, current streak, total stars (anonymous, not linked to any identity).
  • No advertising ID is collected (explicitly disabled).
  • No ad personalization, remarketing, or behavioral profiling is performed.
  • Analytics data is anonymous and aggregated — it cannot be used to identify any individual child.

Website Email List (Optional)

On our website (brushquest.app), visitors may voluntarily provide their email address to receive launch notifications and occasional updates about Brush Quest. This is entirely optional and is not part of the app itself.

  • Email address — used solely to send launch announcements and product updates.
  • We may also record your platform interest (iPhone, Android/Play Store, or general) to send more relevant updates.
  • Email addresses are stored and managed by Buttondown, a privacy-focused email service. Buttondown does not use tracking pixels or cookies by default.
  • Every email includes an unsubscribe link. You can opt out at any time.

4. Children's Privacy (COPPA Compliance)

Brush Quest is designed for children and complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), including the 2025 amendments:

  • We do not collect personal information from children. All data collection features (cloud save, camera) require explicit parental consent.
  • Google Sign-In is intended for parents or guardians only. Access requires passing a parental verification gate (math problem) and explicitly consenting to data storage.
  • The camera feature is disabled by default and requires parental consent to enable. No biometric data is collected — the camera produces only an aggregate motion score that cannot identify any individual.
  • The app does not allow children to make their information publicly available.
  • We do not condition a child's participation on disclosing more information than is reasonably necessary.
  • Firebase Analytics is configured with child-directed treatment: advertising ID collection is disabled, ad personalization is denied, and only anonymous aggregate data is collected. Firebase Analytics uses persistent identifiers (installation IDs) solely for internal operations as permitted under 16 CFR 312.5(c)(7) — these identifiers are not used to contact individuals, build profiles, or serve advertising.

Verifiable Parental Consent

Brush Quest obtains parental consent through the following mechanisms:

  • Parental gate: Settings (including cloud save and camera) are behind a math verification problem that deters unsupervised access by children.
  • Explicit consent dialog: Before enabling cloud save (Google Sign-In), a consent dialog explains what data will be stored and links to this privacy policy. The parent must tap "I Consent" to proceed.
  • Camera consent notice: Before enabling the motion camera, a notice explains the camera's function and confirms no images are stored.

5. Parental Rights

Under COPPA, as a parent or guardian you have the right to:

  • Review your child's data — Game progress is visible on the home screen and in Settings. Cloud data can be viewed by signing in. You may also request a summary of all data associated with your account by emailing brushquest.app@gmail.com.
  • Delete all data — Use the "Delete child's data" option in the app's Settings screen. This erases all local data and all cloud data associated with your account. You may also request deletion by email.
  • Refuse further collection — You can sign out of Google to stop cloud syncing, and disable the motion camera in Settings. The app works fully offline with no data collection beyond anonymous analytics.
  • Disable the camera — Toggle off "Motion Camera" in Settings. The camera is disabled by default.
  • Opt out of analytics — Contact us at brushquest.app@gmail.com to request opt-out from analytics data collection.

We will respond to all parental requests within 30 days.
