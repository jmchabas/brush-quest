## 1. Story Angles (Ranked by Media Appeal)

### Angle A: "Dad Builds Entire App Because His Kid Won't Brush" (PRIMARY)

**Why it works:** This is a human-interest story that writes itself. Every parent has fought the brushing battle. A dad building an entire app because his 7-year-old refused to brush is inherently funny, relatable, and share-worthy. It is emotionally legible in a single headline.

**Best for:** Local TV, parenting publications, morning shows, viral social sharing.

**Key beats to hit:**
- The specific nightly battle scene (what did bedtime look like before?)
- The moment Jim decided to build it ("I was standing in the bathroom at 8pm thinking there has to be a better way")
- Oliver's first reaction when he saw the app
- The reversal: Oliver now ASKS to brush his teeth
- Theo (3) adopted it too — proof it works across ages

**Proof points:** Oliver's transformation from brushing-refuser to brushing-requester. Two kids, ages 3 and 7, both engaged. The app is real, on the Play Store, not a concept.

---

### Angle B: "Solo Dad Uses AI to Build a Kids Health App" (TECH)

**Why it works:** The AI-built-by-one-person narrative is timely and gives tech reporters a concrete, feel-good AI story that is not about replacing workers or existential risk. DALL-E for art, ElevenLabs for voices, Claude for code assistance — one person, entire production team replaced.

**Best for:** TechCrunch, The Verge, Wired, IndieHackers, Hacker News, AI newsletters.

**Key beats to hit:**
- Jim is not a game studio. He is one person.
- The art was generated with DALL-E (monsters, heroes, space backgrounds)
- The voice acting was generated with ElevenLabs (3 distinct character voices, 87+ audio files)
- Claude Code assisted with development
- Total team: 1 founder + AI tools. Total external spend: under $500 in AI credits
- What would have required a 5-10 person team 3 years ago took one person in months

**Hook for tech press:** "What does it look like when one parent uses every available AI tool to build a real product? Not a demo, not a weekend project — a polished app on the Play Store."

---

### Angle C: "The App That Makes Kids BEG to Brush" (PARENTING SOLUTION)

**Why it works:** Parenting publications live on "this thing solved my problem" stories. This angle leads with the outcome parents care about: my kid now wants to brush.

**Best for:** Parents Magazine, Scary Mommy, Today's Parent, Motherly, parenting blogs, parenting TikTok/Reels.

**Key beats to hit:**
- Before: every night was a fight. Tears, negotiations, bribery.
- After: "Mom, can I brush my teeth?"
- The psychology: gamification taps intrinsic motivation. Kids do not brush because they are told to — they brush because they want to evolve their hero and defeat the next monster.
- It is free. No in-app purchases. No ads. No tracking.
- Dentist-recommended 6-zone system with visual mouth guide.

**Proof points:** Two real children (Oliver, 7, and Theo, 3) who demonstrably changed behavior.

---

### Angle D: "7-Year-Old's Toothbrushing Rebellion Inspired a Business" (FAMILY ORIGIN)

**Why it works:** Puts Oliver center-stage. The kid who would not brush is now the company's chief product tester. This angle gives journalists a character arc to write about.

**Best for:** Feature stories, podcasts that do longer interviews, local human-interest segments.

**Key beats to hit:**
- Oliver as the "accidental product manager" — his feedback shaped the app
- Theo as the youngest beta tester (proves age range)
- Family-as-startup: testing at bedtime, feedback over breakfast
- The specific details that only come from real kids (Oliver wanted the monsters to be scarier, insisted on earning stars, the Space Rangers theme was his idea)

---

### Angle E: "Children's Dental Health Month" (SEASONAL HEALTH)

**Why it works:** February is National Children's Dental Health Month (ADA). This gives a hard news peg. Dental publications and health reporters actively look for stories in January-February.

**Best for:** Dental trade publications, health reporters at general outlets, ADA press contacts, local TV health segments.

**Key beats to hit:**
- Childhood cavities are the most common chronic disease in kids (CDC data)
- 1 in 5 children ages 5-11 has at least one untreated cavity
- The brushing habit gap: most kids do not brush long enough or thoroughly enough
- Brush Quest addresses the HOW (guided 6-zone system) and the WHY (motivation through gameplay)

**Timing:** Pitch in early January for February coverage. Can also tie to August (back-to-school dental checkups).

---

### Angle F: "COPPA-Compliant by Design — What a Kids App Should Look Like" (INDUSTRY/POLICY)

**Why it works:** Kids' app privacy is a hot topic. FTC enforcement of COPPA is increasing. Most kids' apps fail basic privacy tests. Brush Quest was designed with no ads, no tracking, no behavioral data, camera processed locally and never uploaded. This is a counter-narrative to the usual "kids' apps are surveillance" stories.

**Best for:** Privacy-focused publications, tech policy reporters, parent advocacy groups (Common Sense Media, Campaign for a Commercial-Free Childhood).

## 5. Local-First Strategy

### Why Local First

1. **Highest acceptance rate.** Local outlets cover local people. Jim is an Alameda resident. That alone is a story qualifier.
2. **Fastest turnaround.** Local papers and TV can run a story within days. National outlets take weeks to months.
3. **Credibility building.** "As seen on ABC7" or "Featured in East Bay Times" transforms later pitches from "unknown founder" to "founder featured in..."
4. **Clip creation.** Every local hit creates a clip, quote, or link that goes into the media kit.
5. **Potential viral pathway.** Local TV clips posted to YouTube/social media regularly go viral when the story is good. A clip of a 7-year-old fighting cavity monsters on a morning show is inherently shareable.

### Local Execution Plan

#### Phase 1: Print/Digital (Weeks 1-2)
1. **Alameda Sun** — email editor directly. Attach headshot, 2 screenshots, 1-paragraph pitch. This should be a near-guaranteed hit (local resident, interesting story, tech angle).
2. **Berkeleyside** — email news desk. East Bay tech + parenting story.
3. **Patch Alameda** — submit through community news. Low effort, low bar.

#### Phase 2: TV (Weeks 2-3)
1. Send tip form to all 5 Bay Area stations simultaneously.
2. In the tip, explicitly offer: "Happy to do a live demo on set with my 7-year-old if that works for your format."
3. Include 2-3 screenshot images and a 15-second video clip in the pitch email.
4. Morning shows plan segments 1-5 days out. Be available on short notice.

#### Phase 3: Radio & Podcasts (Weeks 3-4)
1. KQED pitch (longest lead time of local outlets).
2. Any Alameda/East Bay community podcasts.
3. Local parent group newsletters (Alameda Moms Facebook group, Nextdoor, school listservs — these are not media, but they are earned attention).

#### Phase 4: Community (Ongoing)
1. **Alameda Library** — ask if they feature local creators or host community tech talks.
2. **Local parenting groups** — not as a pitch, but as a genuine share. "Hey, I built this thing for my kid and other parents have been asking about it."
3. **Pediatric dentist offices in Alameda** — leave business cards or flyers (this overlaps with the dental distribution strategy in STRATEGY.md).
4. **Local schools** — PTA newsletters, school health fairs.

### Local to National Bridge

The explicit strategy is:
1. Get 2-3 local clips (print or TV)
2. Add those clips to the media kit
3. Pitch Tier 2 (parenting publications) with: "As featured in [local outlet], here's the story of..."
4. Get 2-3 parenting publication hits
5. Pitch Tier 3 (tech press) with: "Parents Magazine called it... [quote]. Here's the founder story."
6. If any single piece of coverage gets significant social traction, immediately pitch Tier 5 (national) with: "This story about an Alameda dad is blowing up — [link, metrics]"

## 7. Product Hunt & Hacker News Launch

### Product Hunt

#### When to Launch
- **Optimal day:** Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday (highest traffic days)
- **Optimal time:** 12:01 AM PT (Product Hunt resets daily at midnight PT, early posts get the most upvote runway)
- **Prerequisite:** App must be publicly available on Play Store (not internal testing track)

#### Preparation Checklist (Start 2 Weeks Before)

**Assets needed:**
- [ ] Thumbnail image (240x240)
- [ ] Gallery images (5-8 images): app screenshots, hero lineup, before/after family photo
- [ ] Demo video/GIF (30 seconds, autoplay, no sound — PH gallery is silent)
- [ ] Tagline (60 characters max): "Turn your kid's toothbrushing battle into a space adventure"
- [ ] Description (paragraph): Lead with the parent pain point, then the solution
- [ ] First comment from maker: Personal story (Angle A), thank the community, ask for feedback
- [ ] Maker profile: Complete with photo, bio, and link to the app

**Community preparation:**
- [ ] Be active on Product Hunt for 2+ weeks before launch (upvote others, leave genuine comments)
- [ ] Connect with 5-10 Product Hunt "hunters" who focus on family/health/indie products
- [ ] Ask a well-known hunter to hunt the product (this gives it more initial visibility)
- [ ] Identify 20-30 people in your personal + professional network who have PH accounts and will upvote and comment on launch day

**Launch day sequence:**
1. 12:01 AM PT: Product goes live
2. 12:05 AM: Post maker's first comment (personal story, genuine, not salesy)
3. 6:00 AM: Send personal messages to supporters asking them to check it out (not "please upvote" — that violates PH norms)
4. 8:00 AM: Share on X/Twitter, LinkedIn
5. Throughout the day: Respond to EVERY comment on the PH page. Be genuine, answer questions, thank people.
6. End of day: Thank everyone, share results

**What success looks like:**
- Top 5 product of the day = major visibility
- 200+ upvotes = strong showing for an indie product
- Featured in PH daily newsletter = massive exposure
- Any coverage from PH-watching journalists

### Hacker News ("Show HN")

#### When to Post
- **Optimal day:** Tuesday or Wednesday
- **Optimal time:** 8:00-10:00 AM ET (peak HN traffic)
- **Do NOT post on the same day as Product Hunt.** Space them 3-7 days apart to maximize distinct coverage windows.

#### Post Format

```
Show HN: Brush Quest – I built an app because my 7-year-old wouldn't brush his teeth

My son Oliver turned every bedtime into a 20-minute brushing negotiation. After
exhausting every toothbrush and timer on the market, I built him a game where the

Tech stack: Flutter/Dart, DALL-E (monster/hero art), ElevenLabs (3 character

Built solo. No team, no funding. AI tools replaced what would have been
an artist, voice actor, and sound designer.

Result: Oliver now asks to brush. His 3-year-old brother adopted it too.

Free on Google Play, no ads, no tracking, COPPA compliant. Camera processing

[Play Store link]

Happy to answer any questions about the tech, the AI workflow, or the
```

#### HN Success Factors
- **Lead with the human story**, not the product. HN responds to authentic stories.
- **Include technical depth.** HN readers want to know the stack, the tradeoffs, the hard problems solved.
- **Mention AI tools specifically.** This will generate discussion (both supporters and skeptics) which drives engagement.
- **Be present in comments.** Respond to every question within minutes for the first 2 hours. HN rewards active participation.
- **Do not ask for upvotes.** HN will penalize this.
- **Expect tough questions.** HN will ask about privacy, COPPA, AI ethics, monetization. Have answers ready.

## 8. Automation Architecture

## 9. Metrics & Escalation Triggers

### Coverage Tracking Metrics

| Metric | How to Track |
|--------|-------------|
| Pitches sent | Media tracking spreadsheet |
| Response rate | Responses / pitches sent (target: 15-25% for warm, 5-10% for cold) |
| Coverage pieces | Count of published articles/segments/episodes |
| Coverage quality | Tier of outlet (1-5 scale) |
| Referral traffic | Google Analytics on brushquest.app — traffic from press URLs |
| Downloads from coverage | Spike analysis in Play Store Console around coverage dates |
| Social amplification | Shares/reposts of coverage pieces |

### Escalation Triggers: When to Move to the Next Tier

#### Local (Tier 1) --> Parenting Pubs (Tier 2)
**Trigger:** 1-2 published local stories OR a local TV segment.
**What changes in the pitch:** "As featured in [East Bay Times / ABC7]..." — the social proof transforms the pitch from cold to warm-ish.
**When:** Immediately after first local coverage lands.

#### Parenting Pubs (Tier 2) --> Tech Press (Tier 3)
**Trigger:** 2+ parenting publication features OR 500+ Play Store downloads OR notable social media traction (a tweet/post with 1K+ engagements).
**What changes in the pitch:** Include parenting publication quotes, download numbers, user testimonials. The story shifts from "dad's project" to "product gaining traction."
**When:** 2-4 weeks after first parenting coverage.

#### Tech Press (Tier 3) --> Health/Dental (Tier 4)
**Trigger:** Can happen in parallel with Tier 2-3. No prerequisite.
**What changes:** Nothing — dental trade pubs care about the dental health angle, not media credentials. Pitch them on their own timeline, especially around February (Children's Dental Health Month).
**When:** Anytime, but optimal in December-January for February coverage.

#### Any Tier --> National Media (Tier 5)
**Trigger:** One of these:
- A social media post about the app goes viral (50K+ views)
- A local TV clip gets significant online pickup
- Play Store downloads cross 10K with 4.5+ rating
- Multiple Tier 2-3 outlets cover it in the same week (signals trend)
- A celebrity parent or major influencer organically shares the app

**What changes:** Do not cold-pitch national morning shows. Instead:
- Share the coverage package (all clips, links, metrics in one email)
- Frame it as: "This story keeps getting picked up — here's why it resonates"
- Offer the visual: "We can do a live demo with a 7-year-old on set"
- Have video ready (a reel of Oliver using the app, reactions, before/after)

**When:** Only after the evidence is overwhelming. Premature national pitching wastes the opportunity.

### Signals That PR Is Working

| Signal | What It Means | Action |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Journalist replies with questions | Interest, not commitment yet | Reply within 2 hours, answer everything, offer a call |
| Journalist asks for assets/screenshots | Likely writing something | Send immediately, follow up in 3 days |
| Coverage publishes | Win | Amplify on social, add to media kit, thank the journalist |
| Coverage drives measurable downloads | Strong signal | Note which outlet/angle drove downloads. Double down on that angle. |
| Multiple journalists pitch you | You've crossed a threshold | Start being selective. Prioritize highest-reach outlets. |
| No responses after 20 pitches | Angle or targeting issue | Revisit pitch, test new subject lines, try different outlets |

### Time Budget (Weekly)

For a solo founder, PR cannot exceed 3 hours/week sustained. Here is a sustainable allocation:

| Activity | Time | Frequency |
|----------|------|-----------|
| Review and send AI-drafted pitches (2-3 per week) | 30 min | Weekly |
| Check HARO/Connectively for relevant queries | 15 min | Daily (Mon-Fri) |
| Respond to journalist inquiries | 30 min | As needed |
| Social media amplification of any coverage | 15 min | As needed |
| Update tracking spreadsheet | 10 min | Weekly |
| Record podcast episodes (when scheduled) | 45-60 min | Monthly |
| **Total:** | **~2.5 hrs/week** | |

Claude Code handles: drafting pitches, researching journalists, scanning HARO emails, maintaining the tracking spreadsheet, drafting social posts, and preparing for podcast appearances. Jim handles: reviewing, sending, and showing up.
