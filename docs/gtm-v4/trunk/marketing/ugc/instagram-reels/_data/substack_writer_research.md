Also noted: weekly essay cadence; recent testimony before US Senate Commerce Committee (Jan 2026); major theme is "dismantling the EdTech industry."

### Tone / style observations
Advocacy-first, activist register. Uses "tech-intentional™" as a branded phrase. Urgent ("It's time to flip the script," "show the robot to the door"). Personal anecdotes (her own kids' experience) used as evidence. Firm lines, few hedges. She positions herself as a *resistance movement leader*, not a neutral commentator.

### Best-fit pitch angle
**This is the hardest pitch of the three — see red flags.** If you pitch her, the angle must be:
- **Tech-intentional framing:** Brush Quest is a *bounded, short-duration, single-purpose* tool — 2 minutes, offline-capable, no feed, no social, no ads, no data mining. This lines up with her "Four Norms" framework (if you haven't read it, read it before pitching).
- **Screen-minimizing, not screen-maximizing:** The pitch must foreground that the goal is to *end the session in 2 minutes*, not extend engagement. Contrast explicitly with infinite-scroll kids' apps.
- **Parent-authority, not kid-dopamine:** Frame it as a tool the parent controls, not a dopamine hit for the kid.
- Even better: pitch her the *decision criteria* ("how should parents evaluate a kids' app like this?") and let her apply her own framework — she'll want to do the analysis, not take a product's word.

### Red flags / traps to avoid
- **Her entire brand is criticism of apps marketed to kids.** She has publicly testified that EdTech products harm children. A "fun app for kids" pitch will be rejected or, worse, used as a cautionary example.
- She has not (per visible archive) written specifically against gamified health apps, toothbrushing apps, or habit apps — **genuine whitespace**, but also means she may be *primed* to write critically if the pitch lands wrong.
- "Gamification," "engagement," "streaks," "rewards" are near-certainly trigger words in her vocabulary. Rewrite the pitch without them. Say "2-minute timer with a cartoon," not "gamified experience."
- Do not mention Firebase/cloud sync/any data collection without immediately addressing what's stored and why. She is COPPA-literate and will check.
- Paywall: some content appears to be free (posts above confirmed accessible); weekly essay format suggests most is free. "The Reckoning" series exists as a gated section — assume she treats subscribers as the inner circle.

### Confidence note
**High on posts and stance; medium on contact flow.** Post titles/URLs/dates from firstfish.substack.com/archive on 2026-04-20. Contact is form-only (no email exposed) — confirmed by visiting contact page. Stance is unambiguous from archive + search results (Senate testimony, "dismantling EdTech" tagline).

**Recommendation:** Rank this as the highest-risk / highest-ceiling pitch of the three. If you land it, she'd be a credible voice saying "here's an app I actually think is fine" — powerful. If you misfire, she could publicly name Brush Quest as a negative example.
