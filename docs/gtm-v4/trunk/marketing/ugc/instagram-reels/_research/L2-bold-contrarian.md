# L2 — Bold / Contrarian

**Question:** Produce a complete set of agent-executable PRDs to get 50 qualified parent installs to Play Store internal testing in 14 days via organic Instagram Reels.

---

## 1. The default plan (what everyone would do)

The median founder ships: (a) a brand IG handle (@brushquest), (b) 14 founder-POV Reels ("I built an app so my kid would stop fighting me at bedtime"), (c) 5–10 "dollar-spray" DMs to mom-influencers offering a free code. They'll spend 14 days making polished hero content, average 400–900 views per Reel, eat a ~1–2% install-intent rate on a cold-traffic Reel→bio-link→Play-internal-tester funnel, and finish with 8–15 installs. Then they'll blame "the algorithm."

## 2. The asymmetric bet candidates

### Bet A — The "One Creator, Paid for a Batch" play
- **The bet:** Skip the brand handle. Pay ONE mid-tier mom-of-a-7-year-old creator (30–150K followers, toothbrushing/bedtime niche) a flat $400–$600 for a **batch of 3 Reels over 14 days** featuring her real kid using Brush Quest, with a Play internal-tester link in her bio for the run.
- **Why it's 10× potential:** Her audience is pre-qualified parents with kids in the exact age band. One 80K-view Reel from a trusted creator converts to installs at 3–6% (vs. <1% from a cold brand account with no history). That single Reel can deliver the entire 50-install goal on its own. You also get 3 shots on goal for one negotiation, not 3 negotiations.
- **Why it's contrarian:** The brief says "organic." Founders interpret that as "no paid creators." But paid-creator content that a creator **organically posts on their own feed** is still organic reach in the algorithm — Instagram doesn't know or care about the handshake. Meanwhile everyone else spends 14 days building a brand handle from zero against a cold-start penalty.
- **Cheap proxy test (<$200, <4 days):** Spend $120 on a **single** paid post with a micro-creator (10–30K followers, toothbrushing/bedtime niche, usingmodash.io or manual DM-scouting). Track the 72-hour install count + bio-link CTR. If that single post delivers ≥8 installs, scale to the $400–600 batch deal. If it delivers <3, the thesis is wrong — don't scale.

### Bet B — The "Parent Confessional" format weapon
- **The bet:** Commit 10 of 14 Reels to a single stolen-from-comedy format: "Things I said tonight that I didn't know I'd say as a parent" — lip-synced or on-screen text, each one ending with the brushing fight as the capstone absurdity, then a 2-second app clip. No hero-shot "my product is great" Reels at all.
- **Why it's 10× potential:** Reels saves + shares are the algorithm's distribution multiplier, and **relatable-parent-frustration content gets saves 5–15× higher than product-demo content**. A single saved Reel at 50K reach delivers more qualified install intent than 10 polished demo Reels at 5K reach each. Format-native always beats ad-native on organic Reels.
- **Why it's contrarian:** Most founders instinctively make "product explainer" Reels because that's what the product deserves. The algorithm doesn't care what the product deserves. It rewards format adherence to whatever is currently trending in the feed. "Parent confessionals" and "mom-reality-check" formats are evergreen on Reels, Brush Quest has never tried to hijack one.
- **Cheap proxy test (<$0, <5 days):** Post 3 Reels using this format from a brand-new throwaway handle (not @brushquest) over 5 days. If median reach per Reel is ≥2K (meaning the algo likes the format at cold-start), green-light as the dominant content strategy. If median <500, the format doesn't pop from zero and Bet A becomes the higher-EV path.

### Bet C — The "Reddit-first, Reel-second" asymmetric hack
- **The bet:** Recruit 50 qualified parents primarily from **r/toddlers, r/Parenting, r/daddit** (honest "I built this, free internal-tester codes, would love feedback" posts), and use Reels as the credibility layer — i.e., the IG handle exists so Reddit parents who google you find something real. Reels here is a trust asset, not a funnel.
- **Why it's 10× potential:** Reddit parent subs convert "free internal-tester beta" posts at 15–40% when the founder is honest and the ask is small. 50 installs in 14 days from Reddit is very achievable with 4–6 authentic posts; Reels alone makes it a coin-flip. This beat the posed question: the goal is 50 qualified installs, not 50 Reels-sourced installs.
- **Why it's contrarian:** The question scopes us to Reels. The bold move is to notice that the scoping itself may be the trap — if the real goal is 50 installs in 14 days, the EV-max channel is not Reels. Reels stays as a 3-post-deep "we're real" trust layer.
- **Cheap proxy test (<$0, <3 days):** Post one honest "I'm a solo dad-founder, here's my Brush Quest Android internal-tester link, looking for 20 parents with kids 4–8" thread on r/daddit. Measure installs + qualified comments in 48 hours. If ≥12 installs, Reddit becomes the primary acquisition channel and Reels pivots to trust-layer content.

## 3. The uncomfortable truth

The safe plan's slow death is that **a brand-new @brushquest IG handle with 0 followers will be algorithmically suppressed during the entire 14-day window.** Reels cold-start penalty is 2–4 weeks minimum. You'll ship 14 Reels, each averaging <500 views, and convince yourself the format or hook was wrong — when the real issue is that organic Reels at your account age is a physics problem, not a craft problem. Refuse the trap of "just make better Reels." Either borrow someone else's warm-start account (Bet A), or route around Reels entirely (Bet C).

## 4. Ranked takeaway

1. **Bet A (paid micro-creator batch)** — 70% chance of delivering 30+ installs, 30% chance of delivering 80+. Beats the conservative counter-bet of "build brand handle + post 14 Reels."
2. **Bet C (Reddit-first with Reels as trust layer)** — 60% chance of hitting 50 installs inside 14 days at near-$0 cost. Beats the counter-bet of "stay disciplined to Reels-only."
3. **Bet B (confessional format)** — 30% chance of a breakout Reel, 70% chance of mid reach. Best as the *content spec* that Bet A's creator executes, not as standalone.

**Recommended stack:** Run Bet C's Reddit proxy test Day 1. Run Bet A's $120 micro-creator proxy test Day 2–5. Use Bet B as the creative brief handed to whoever (creator or founder) makes the Reels. The PRDs should be written so an executor agent can run all three in parallel from day 1 and reallocate budget by day 6 based on proxy-test results.

## 5. Quick failure modes

- **Confusing "contrarian" with "stupid":** Abandoning Reels entirely is not bold, it's scope-violation. Use Bet C as a *hedge*, not a *pivot* — the GTM factory needs the Reels PRDs either way.
- **Creator-selection error:** Paying a $600 creator with a lifestyle audience that's 60% childless women is worse than $0. Bet A requires ruthless audience-fit verification (ask for screenshot of follower-age/gender breakdown before payment) or it's just burning $600.
- **COPPA blowback from UGC:** Any creator Reel featuring their own kid's face is legally fine (it's *their* child, *their* choice), but do NOT repost it to @brushquest without written consent. The PRD must hard-code a no-repost-without-written-release rule or you will ship a quiet legal landmine on day 12.
