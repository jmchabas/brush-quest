# E2 — Target Parent (ICP) eval of Synth-v1

## 1. Gut reaction

Honestly? If the top Tier-1 thing is a Reddit post in r/androidtesting or r/daddit, that does not reach me. I am not on Reddit at 9pm. I am on Instagram half-watching Love Island, and if something scrolled past me, the 2-second impression has to be a tired mom or dad saying something real about brushing teeth — not a logo, not a hero, not a Space Ranger. The plan basically admits Reels won't do the job in 14 days, which is actually refreshingly honest, but it means the thing labeled "Instagram Reels factory" barely reaches me through Instagram.

## 2. Moments of trust and bounce

**T1a — Reddit posts.** Trust: zero for me, I don't go there. Might reach my husband. Bounce: n/a, wouldn't see it.

**T1b — Micro-creator paid proxy.** Trust: high IF it's a real parent I already half-follow doing a "things my 7yo hates" bit and casually showing the app working. I'd stop. Bounce: the second it feels like a sponsored #ad with the hero-character swooping in and a voiceover about "Space Rangers defeat cavity monsters," I'm out. That's the screen-time-trap reflex. Also if the creator is a picture-perfect mom in a white kitchen holding the phone up like a QVC host — bounce.

**T1c — brushquest.app/rangers landing + email-to-auto-enroll.** Trust: enormous once I'm there, IF the page respects my time. Screenshots, 3 taps, done. Bounce: the phrase "join our Google group to become an internal tester" will kill me instantly. I don't know what that is and I'm not signing up for a group. Fix that wording or I'm gone in 4 seconds.

**T1d — Buttondown + PTA.** Trust: highest of anything here. If another 2nd-grade mom tells me at pickup "my kid actually brushes now," I install that night. Bounce: almost none — unless the email looks like a newsletter template with stock art. Then it feels like spam from the PTA president.

**T2a — throwaway-handle format test.** Neutral. If it hits my feed as a real person confession, fine. If it's obviously a brand account pretending to be a person, that's the exact thing that makes me delete apps.

**T2c — pediatric dentist QR poster.** Trust: pretty high actually. I'm in that waiting room already feeling guilty about my kid's brushing. That's the moment.

**T2d — Substack pitches (Parent Data, etc).** Trust: very high if Emily Oster's orbit even mentions it. I read those. Big if.

**T2f — Facebook mom groups.** Bounce. Those groups are 80% MLM and "does anyone have a pediatrician rec." I skim them; I don't install from them.

## 3. The copy test

The implied hook is the "things I said tonight as a parent" confessional. That can work. What would actually make me stop: a hands-only shot of a parent holding a phone, voice over: "My kid used to scream every single night when I said 'brush your teeth.' Now he asks for it. I don't know how to feel about it." Then 3 seconds of the game, hands of a kid (no face), monster gets zapped. Caption: "Android only for now. Link in bio — takes 3 taps." That lands. "Space Rangers vs Cavity Monsters" does not land on me — that's pitch-deck language. I don't care what the theme is, I care that the screaming stopped.

## 4. What a real parent would actually share

**Would share:** the PTA / 2nd-grade-mom email (T1d). That is literally how every app my kid uses got on my phone. "Oh Beth said her kid will actually brush now, she sent me the link." That's it. That's the whole business.

**Would never share:** the Reddit thread. Parents do not forward Reddit posts to other parents. And nobody is forwarding a brand IG Reel from @brushquest with 400 followers. The warm-up Reels on the brand handle are not a shareable asset; they're wallpaper.

## 5. Strengthen

1. **Kill "internal tester" language everywhere a parent sees it.** Call it "early access." The words "Google group" should never appear on my screen. This is one copy change worth more than half the creator budget.
2. **Make the creator brief explicit that the kid's meltdown is the hook, not the hero.** Right now the plan says "hands-only, parent-confessional," which is right, but the creative spec needs to BAN the Space Ranger aesthetic from the first 3 seconds of any Reel aimed at me. Put the game reveal at second 6, not second 1.
3. **Weaponize T1d harder.** The PTA / school-mom path is the highest-trust, lowest-cost lane in the whole portfolio and it's buried under Reddit and creator stuff. Give Jim a 3-line text message template he can send to Oliver's class parents tonight. Not a newsletter. A text. That's what actually travels.

## 6. Overall verdict

In the best-case Tier-1 version — a real parent on IG I already follow does a tired-confessional, I tap through to a landing page that doesn't say "Google group" and shows me 3 screenshots — yes, I install. Maybe not tonight, but by the weekend.
