# E2 — Target Parent (ICP) Evaluation

Parent of a 6yo who fights me every night about brushing. 9pm, show on in the background, phone in hand. Already deleted the reading app, the mood app, the "screen time that's actually good for them" app. Skeptical.

---

## 1. Gut reaction

If the Tier-1 hit me cold on Instagram — a warm, slightly-funny ad about the nightly toothbrushing fight with a kid's hand holding a toothbrush (not a face) — I'd slow down. For two seconds. Then my thumb is moving again unless the next frame tells me it's free to try and won't ambush me with subscriptions. The 2-second impression I need: "oh, this one isn't trying to turn my kid into a Duolingo addict."

## 2. Moments of trust and bounce

**Pillar 1 (retention dashboard + ASO):** Doesn't touch me at all — this is founder plumbing. Trust builds only because the store listing will look less janky. Fine. Invisible.

**Pillar 2 (pediatric dentist):** Trust: real. If Dr. Liu hands my kid a card with a cartoon monster on it and says "try this, it helped the Johnson kid," I download it in the parking lot. That recommendation carries more weight than any ad. Bounce: a QR code taped to a waiting room wall next to five other flyers? I won't notice it. I'm refereeing my kid and checking work Slack. **The dentist has to actually hand it to me or mention it out loud.** A passive flyer is wallpaper.

**Pillar 3 (earned media + Editor's Choice + Common Sense Media):** Trust: Common Sense Media is the only one that moves me. That's the badge I actually check before installing — it's muscle memory at this point. Google Play Editor's Choice is a nice decoration but I don't scroll the Play Store; I search by name after someone tells me. Romper/Fatherly — I'd read that article if it showed up in my feed but I wouldn't go looking. Bounce: "solo founder built this with AI agents" is a cool Hacker News story, not a parent pitch. Please don't lead with it.

**Pillar 4 (newsletter sponsorships):** Trust: high if it's a newsletter I already read (Big Little Feelings, Lucie's List, something my mom friends forward). Bounce: if it's a random Substack I've never heard of, I scroll past sponsor blocks like ads.

**T2-A (Cavity Monster song):** My 6yo does not come home singing TikTok songs to me. He comes home asking for whatever his friend Jaxon has. **Character-IP that reaches the parent through the kid requires the kid to hear it first, and my kid hears YouTube Kids and the school bus.** TikTok isn't the vector. If the song ends up on a Ms. Rachel-style channel, different story.

**T2-C (Reddit):** I do not read r/Parenting. The moms I know do not read r/Parenting. This reaches childless people with opinions about parenting.

**T2-E (micro-creator gifting):** The dental-pro creators could actually land. A parent-creator showing her kid not screaming at the sink — I'd screenshot that.

## 3. The copy test

The implied hook "gamified 2-min timer, defeat cavity monsters, earn stars" — no. That's a product spec.

What lands: **"For the parent who's been negotiating with a 6-year-old over a toothbrush for four years. Two minutes. No fight. No ads, no subscription, no asking your kid to buy stuff."** Say the ugly thing first. Every parent knows the fight. Acknowledge it, then tell me you won't re-sell it to my kid.

## 4. What a real parent would actually share vs. not

**Would share organically:** mom-text-chain after it actually works twice. "ok this toothbrush app is unhinged but he brushed for two full minutes without crying, sending the link." That's the only route that matters.

**Would never share:** a Substack post. A CSM badge. A Reddit thread. An Editor's Choice ranking. I have never once texted a friend a Common Sense Media review in my life.

## 5. Strengthen

1. **Make the dentist play active, not passive.** Flyers die. A small card the hygienist hands the kid with a sticker and a QR — that the hygienist says out loud — is a different product. Script the handoff, don't just ship paper.
2. **Put CSM approval on the store listing screenshot itself.** Don't just "submit to CSM" as a distant press play. Whatever badge comes out of it has to be visible at the install-decision moment.
3. **Seed the mom-text-chain directly.** Find 20 real parents in 3 cities, give them the app, ask them — after their kid uses it twice — to text one friend. That's the organic loop. Everything else is noise around it.

## 6. Verdict

If a dentist hands it to me and a friend texts me the same week, yes, I install. If Pillar 1 is the plan as-is running cold, I probably don't notice it.
