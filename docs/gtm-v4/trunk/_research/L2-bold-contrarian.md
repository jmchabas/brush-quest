# L2 — Bold / Contrarian — Trunk Portfolio

## 1. The default plan (what everyone would do)

The median founder spends $1–2K across three tired channels: ~$600 Meta ads
to parents of 4–8yo's, ~$400 boosting an Instagram Reels portfolio with 6–10
creators doing "honest mom" reviews, and ~$400 chasing a couple of parenting
newsletter sponsorships (The Sunday Chaos, Big Little Feelings adjacent). They
optimize CPI, celebrate at $2.50 installs, and land roughly 400–700 installs
with ~20–30% W1 activation = 100–200 WAK. They miss goal by 5–10×. This is
the plan 80% of kids-app founders are shipping right now, and it is a slow,
respectable failure.

## 2. The asymmetric bet candidates

### Bet A — The "Cavity Monster" IP play (creator-driven character fandom)

- **The bet:** Spend $600 to license/commission 3–5 original Cavity Monster
  character songs (suno-style or indie kid-musician on Fiverr/Upwork) + a
  pack of 30 short Lottie/GIF reaction stickers for WhatsApp/iMessage, then
  seed them to 10 "toddler meme" IG/TikTok accounts (500K–3M followers each)
  in exchange for exclusive first-use rights, not cash.
- **Why it's 10× potential:** Character IP has exponential reach: a viral
  Monster song runs 5–50M plays, and each play is a parent-facing earworm
  that routes through `brushquest.app/rangers`. One hit song = all 1,000 WAK
  and a defensible moat. Cocomelon, Bluey, Ms. Rachel all prove kids-music
  distribution collapses CAC by 20–100× vs paid installs.
- **Why it's contrarian:** Every app founder assumes "we're a product, not
  a media company." Wrong. Kids app = media business first, software second.
  Nobody downloads a utility toothbrush app; they download the monsters their
  kid already loves. The conventional playbook says "focus on product, buy
  installs." The asymmetric play is to build the monsters into culture before
  the install is even considered.
- **Cheap proxy test (<$150, 5 days):** Pay $75 on Fiverr for one 45-second
  "Plaque Attack" song + one Monster dance clip. Post as raw audio on TikTok
  with #kidssongs. If it pulls >25K plays in 72h on a new account, the
  thesis is live. If <5K, kill and redirect.

### Bet B — The "Brush Club" pediatric dentist drop-ship

- **The bet:** Mail 300 branded "Cavity Monster Hunter Kits" (printed QR
  card + sticker sheet + 1 page parent handout, ~$3 unit all-in = $900) to
  pediatric dentists we hand-pick from Google Maps in 5 metro areas, with a
  one-page "give these to parents at checkout" ask. No cash, no affiliate,
  just "free thing for your waiting room."
- **Why it's 10× potential:** Pediatric dentists have the highest-intent
  distribution on Earth for this exact product — a parent holding a freshly
  lectured child about cavities, at the exact moment of maximum receptiveness.
  If 50 of 300 dentists hand out 20 kits each = 1,000 qualified parent
  impressions. Even 10% activation = 100 WAK from ONE kit run. Rinse-repeat
  monthly at breakeven LTV.
- **Why it's contrarian:** This looks "offline and slow" — the exact kind of
  thing a Twitter growth hacker mocks. But it's the only channel where the
  prescriber, parent, and purchase-intent collide. Dentists also solve the
  trust problem agentic digital cannot: a kids-health app recommended by a
  pediatric dentist skips 6 months of brand-building.
- **Cheap proxy test (<$80, 1 week):** Print 20 kits ($30) + postage ($20)
  + 4 cold emails/calls to local Portland/LA pediatric dentists. If 2 of 4
  say yes to stocking, scale. If 0 of 4, the channel is dead.

### Bet C — The "Tuck-In Challenge" co-marketing swap with sleep/bedtime apps

- **The bet:** Negotiate in-app cross-promotion with 2–3 non-competing
  parent-audience apps (Moshi Sleep, Slumberkins, Hatch Rest, Yoto Player
  content partners) — they feature Brush Quest in their bedtime-routine
  flow, we embed their "next step" after Victory screen. Zero cash,
  revenue-share or traffic-swap only.
- **Why it's 10× potential:** Those apps have millions of already-activated
  parents in the exact routine context ("we're brushing teeth and going to
  bed"). One partnership with a 1M-DAU sleep app placing us in their
  bedtime checklist = 10K+ qualified taps per week, zero CAC.
- **Why it's contrarian:** Founders assume no one bigger will talk to them.
  Moshi & co. actually *need* content partners in their routine story and
  trade generously. The time cost (Jim, ~4 hrs of cold outreach) is the real
  budget. Nobody does this because it feels like begging.
- **Cheap proxy test (<$0, 3 days):** Send 10 founder-to-founder emails
  with a 1-pager. Target ≥2 "let's talk" replies. If <1, the thesis is cold.

## 3. The uncomfortable truth

The safe plan's real killer is not CPI — it's that **Meta ads to parents of
4–8yo's are priced for apps monetizing at $40+ LTV** (subscription language
apps, $9.99/mo ABCmouse). Brush Quest's $9.99 one-time + 10–20% conversion
= ~$1–2 LTV. You will lose $1.50 per install forever, forever. Running ads
on a $1–2K budget against apps burning $50K/mo is playing their game at
1/25th scale. The slow death is spending 90 days proving a channel that is
structurally impossible before you try a channel that isn't.

## 4. Ranked takeaway (expected value)

1. **Bet A (Character IP)** — ~25% hit chance × 50× upside = highest EV.
   Beats: boosted Reels creator pool.
2. **Bet B (Pediatric dentist)** — ~50% hit chance × 5× upside = steady EV,
   zero downside. Beats: parenting newsletter sponsorships.
3. **Bet C (Partnership swap)** — ~30% hit chance × 10× upside, mostly Jim's
   time. Beats: Meta paid installs.

Run all three proxies in week 1 ($230 total). Double-down on whichever pings.

## 5. Quick failure modes

- **Contrarian ≠ stupid.** If Bet A's first song flops, don't triple-down on
  "more songs" — the thesis (character-first media) failed, not the budget.
- **IP loves law.** Original character songs need clean licensing; don't
  copy existing melodies (Baby Shark-style lawsuits kill kids apps).
- **Dentist channel has a real ceiling.** 300 kits/month scales to ~300 WAK,
  not 10K. It's a floor-builder, not a moonshot — don't mistake reliability
  for asymmetry.
