Brush Quest

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 14, 2026

Brush Quest is a children's toothbrushing game designed for kids aged 7 and older. We take your child's privacy seriously. This policy explains what information the app uses, how it is handled, and your rights as a parent or guardian.

The short version: Brush Quest has no ads, no in-app purchases, no location tracking, and no behavioral profiling. We collect anonymous usage analytics (e.g. brush completion rates) to improve the app, with no advertising IDs or personal data. We do not sell or share personal data with anyone.

1. What Information We Collect and Why

Camera (Motion Detection)

Brush Quest can use the device's front camera to detect brushing motion during gameplay. This feature is optional and can be toggled off in Settings.

  • Camera frames are processed entirely on the device in real time.
  • No images or video are ever stored, recorded, or transmitted.
  • No facial recognition or identification is performed.

Google Sign-In (Optional)

A parent or guardian may choose to sign in with a Google account to enable cloud saving. When signed in, we access:

  • Email address — used solely to identify the user's cloud save data.
  • Display name — shown on the settings screen so the parent can confirm which account is connected.

No profile photo, contacts, or other Google account data is accessed.

Game Progress Data

The app stores gameplay information to track your child's brushing habits and in-game rewards:

  • Total brush count and brush history (dates only)
  • Current streak and best streak
  • Stars earned
  • Unlocked heroes and weapons
  • Selected hero and weapon
  • Settings preferences (timer duration, sound, camera toggle)

Crash Reports (Firebase Crashlytics)

The app collects anonymous crash reports to help us fix bugs and improve stability. These reports include:

  • Device model and operating system version
  • Stack traces (technical error details)
  • App version

Crash reports contain no personal information such as names, emails, or gameplay data.

Usage Analytics (Firebase Analytics)

The app collects anonymous usage data to help us understand how children use the app and improve the experience. This is configured for child-directed treatment under COPPA:

  • Events tracked: Brush session start/completion/abandonment, onboarding completion, daily logins, shop visits, hero and weapon unlocks.
  • Aggregate properties: Lifetime brush count, current streak, total stars (anonymous, not linked to any identity).
  • No advertising ID is collected (explicitly disabled).
  • No ad personalization, remarketing, or behavioral profiling is performed.
  • Analytics data is anonymous and aggregated — it cannot be used to identify any individual child.

2. How Data Is Stored and Protected

Data Storage Location Details
Game progress On device Stored via SharedPreferences (Android). Protected by device-level security (lock screen, encryption).
Cloud save Google Firebase (US) Only if a parent signs in. Stored in Cloud Firestore. Each user can only access their own data.
Crash reports Google Firebase (US) Anonymous, auto-collected. Retained by Firebase Crashlytics per Google's data retention policies.
Usage analytics Google Firebase (US) Anonymous event data (brush completions, app engagement). No advertising IDs. Child-directed treatment enabled. Retained per Firebase Analytics' default policies.
Camera frames Not stored Processed in device memory only. Never saved or transmitted.

Firebase data is protected by Google's infrastructure security, including encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest. Firestore security rules ensure each user can only read and write their own document.

We do not use any advertising SDKs, social media integrations, or any external services beyond Firebase. Firebase Analytics is configured with child-directed treatment (no advertising IDs, no ad personalization).

4. Children's Privacy (COPPA Compliance)

Brush Quest is designed for children and complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA):

  • We do not collect personal information from children.
  • Google Sign-In is intended for parents or guardians only. The sign-in process uses a parent verification gate (math problem) to deter unsupervised use by children.
  • The app does not allow children to make their information publicly available.
  • We do not condition a child's participation on disclosing more information than is reasonably necessary.
  • Firebase Analytics is configured with child-directed treatment: advertising ID collection is disabled, ad personalization is denied, and only anonymous aggregate data is collected.

5. Parental Rights

As a parent or guardian, you have the right to:

  • Review your child's data — Game progress is visible on the home screen and in Settings. Cloud data can be viewed by signing in.
  • Delete all data — Use the "Reset All Progress" option in the app's Settings screen. This erases all local data. To delete cloud data, contact us at the email below.
  • Opt out of cloud sync — Simply do not sign in with Google. The app works fully offline.
  • Disable the camera — Toggle off "Motion Camera" in Settings. The app works without it.
  • Request data deletion — Contact us to request complete deletion of any cloud-stored data associated with your account.

6. Data Retention

  • Local data is retained on the device until the app is uninstalled or progress is manually reset in Settings.
  • Cloud data (Firestore) is retained as long as the user account exists. You may request deletion at any time by contacting us.
  • Crash reports are retained per Firebase Crashlytics' default retention period (90 days).

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will update the effective date at the top of this page. We encourage parents to review this policy periodically.

Continued use of the app after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
